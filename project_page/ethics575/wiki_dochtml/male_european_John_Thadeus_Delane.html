    John Thadeus Delane

    1John Thadeus Delane He was the second son of W. F. A. Delane , a barrister , of an old Irish family , who about 1832 was appointed by Times publisher John Walter II to financial manager of The Times .
    2In 1841 he succeeded Thomas Barnes as editor , a post which he occupied for thirty-six years .
    3He admired Palmerston and respected Lord Aberdeen , and was of considerable use to both ; and it was Lord Aberdeen himself who , in 1845 , told him of the impending repeal of the Corn Laws , an incident round which many incorrect stories have gathered .
    4The history , however , of the events during the thirteen administrations , between then and 1877 , in which The Times , and therefore Delane , played an important part can not here be recapitulated .
