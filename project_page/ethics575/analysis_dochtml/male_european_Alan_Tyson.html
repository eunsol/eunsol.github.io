    Alan Tyson, 74, Psychiatrist Noted for Musical Scholarship

    1Alan Tyson , a British musicologist and psychoanalyst whose works on Haydn , Mozart and Beethoven over the last 40 years are considered among the most important achievements of modern musical scholarship , died on Nov. 10 at Shoreham-by-Sea , in Sussex , England .
    2He was 74 .
    3The cause was Alzheimer 's disease , according to The Daily Telegraph of London .
    4Starting in the early 1960 's , Dr. Tyson published articles showing , for example , that two piano trios attributed to Haydn were actually by Ignaz Pleyel , and that the most familiar edition of the Mozart Horn Concertos ( K. 412 ) had been embellished with the work of later composers .
    5In a 1962 essay on Beethoven 's Violin Concerto , he showed that the standard published text of the work was not reliable , and published a corrected , definitive edition .

    7His analysis of Beethoven 's sketchbooks helped establish their proper order and illuminated their creator 's compositional methods .

    16He also did important work on the music of Muzio Clementi and John Field .
